# Sources for SKILL-SV.md (Swedish)

Created: 2026-06-16

All research was conducted in Swedish. No pattern from the English original (avoid-ai-writing by Conor Bronsdon) or from SKILL-DE.md was transferred without verification against independent Swedish-language sources.

## Sources Used

### 1. Erigo – "Skriver din kollega, eller är det ChatGPT" (main source)

**Author:** Katri Lindgren
**URL:** https://erigo.se/sv/artiklar/skriver-din-kollega-eller-ar-det-chatgpt-och-spelar-det-nagon-roll
**Date:** 27 April 2026
**Type:** Swedish-native practitioner essay ("AI-text på svenska: 10 markörer, 6 mekanismer, 7 tips")
**Relevance:**
- The single richest Swedish-native analysis found. Not a translation; written by a Swedish practitioner observing Swedish LLM output.
- Documents ten concrete markers: (1) em-dash everywhere, (2) the banal negation "Det handlar inte om X, utan om Y", (3) "weight without weight" ("Det är avgörande", "Det är viktigt att notera", "Värt att uppmärksamma"), (4) encouraging framing / validation / pep, (5) rhetorical announcement ("Här är grejen", "Tänk på det så här"), (6) the rule of three, (7) hedging as default ("ofta", "kan", "tenderar att", "i många fall", "i viss mån"), (8) the repeating summary ("Sammanfattningsvis", "för att knyta ihop det"), (9) the travel metaphor ("navigera landskapet", "på din resa", "väva in i vardagen", "steg på vägen"), (10) the even pulse / uniform rhythm.
- Documents value-loading mechanism: "viktig", "avgörande", "kritisk", "kraftfull", "fascinerande" are high-frequency and reflexively chosen — "Om allt är avgörande är inget avgörande."

**Documented patterns:** #2 (value-loading), #8 (Tier 1/2 vocab), #11 (banal negation), #13 (travel metaphor), #15a (hedging), #17 (false contrast), #25a (rhetorical announcement), #30 (summary openers), #31 (emphasis markers), #32 (encouraging framing), #36a (rule of three), #37 (even pulse), #40 (repeating summary); Tier 1 (avgörande, kraftfull, fascinerande, kritisk, genomgripande), Tier 3 ("det är viktigt att notera", "värt att uppmärksamma", "sammanfattningsvis")

### 2. SEO.se / Noor Digital – "Funkar AI-detektorer? Så upptäcker du AI-text"

**Authors:** Erla Andersson (Sr. SEO Copywriter), Navid Hashemi (ML Specialist)
**URL:** https://seo.se/upptack-ai-text/
**Date:** Updated 12 Dec 2025
**Type:** Swedish SEO agency blog
**Relevance:**
- Provides an explicit Swedish list of "de förstörda orden" (the ruined words): *oumbärligt, navigera, avgörande, noggrann, korrekt, utforska, upptäck, vital, välrenommerad, designad för att, välinformerad, idealiskt, inte bara /…/ utan, sammanfattningsvis* — and the em-dash (tankstreck).
- Documents perplexity/burstiness, generic "bläh" tone, and the "ryggen fri" hedging pattern ("Det kan bli si och så", "Det är alltid bäst att kontakta en specialist").

**Documented patterns:** Tier 1 (oumbärlig, avgörande, vital, välrenommerad), Tier 2 (navigera, utforska, upptäck, noggrann, korrekt, idealisk, välinformerad, designad för att), #15a (hedging), #17 (false contrast "inte bara… utan"), #18 (em-dash), #37 (uniform rhythm)

### 3. Pedagog Malmö – "Kan man upptäcka ai-genererade texter?"

**URL:** https://pedagog.malmo.se/bloggposter/kan-man-upptacka-ai-genererade-texter/
**Type:** Swedish municipal education resource (City of Malmö teaching portal)
**Relevance:**
- Documents a specifically Swedish ChatGPT habit: the expression "det är viktigt att" followed by a word on the theme "notera / komma ihåg / lägga på minnet".
- Documents the pattern of a positive adjective (fantastiska / imponerande / magnifika) followed by geographic origin (Arktis / arktiska regionen) — observed across repeated generations on the same topic.
- Documents uniform paragraph division, and the fact that ChatGPT (unlike humans) does not start several paragraphs with the same word.

**Documented patterns:** #19 (uniform paragraphs), #31 (emphasis markers "det är viktigt att notera"), #41 (adjective + geographic origin)

### 6. Språktidningen – "Här stinker texterna AI lång väg" (Ulrika Good)

**Author:** Ulrika Good (copywriter, 30+ years)
**URL:** https://spraktidningen.se/kronikor/har-stinker-texterna-ai-lang-vag/
**Type:** Established Swedish language magazine (authority source)
**Relevance:**
- Documents the em-dash (tankstreck) as having had a "record increase" and being a clear AI marker — explicitly notes the long em-dash "previously not seen in Swedish contexts, only in English-language publications."
- Documents that AI is trained on American texts and carries an American tonality "far from our Swedish, considerably more sparse temperament" — basis for the English-calque and inflated-tone observations.
- Documents LinkedIn as the densest channel for Swedish AI "brag posts", and the habit of starting each paragraph with an "over-pedagogical emoji" (lightbulb for a point, warning triangle for risks, happy smiley for the positive close).

**Documented patterns:** #18 (em-dash, Swedish-specific origin), #20 (emoji per paragraph), #17a (English calques / American tonality), example context (LinkedIn)

## Gaps and Undocumented Patterns

The following were treated with caution because no dedicated Swedish-language source was found documenting them as statistically over-represented in Swedish LLM output specifically:

1. **Dedicated Swedish Wikipedia community guide.** No equivalent of the English "Wikipedia:Signs of AI writing" was found on sv.wikipedia.org. Only Swedish *news coverage* of the (English-language) WikiProject AI Cleanup was available. Patterns #4 and #7 rest on that coverage, not on a Swedish editor field guide.

2. **Copula avoidance beyond "spelar en (avgörande) roll".** Only "spelar en avgörande roll" is directly sourced (Mind the Graph). The other verbs listed in pattern #9 ("fungerar som", "utgör", "är att betrakta som") are practitioner inference by analogy with the documented English/German pattern, not independently sourced for Swedish.

3. **Synonym chains (#10).** General LLM behaviour; no Swedish-specific corpus study found.

4. **No published frequency study for Swedish.** Unlike German (Schaaff & Schlippe, IU International, 2023, which covers EN/FR/DE/ES but *not* Swedish), no academic paper was found giving sample sizes and word-frequency comparisons for Swedish AI vs human text. The Tier-1/2 claims rest on practitioner consensus across sources 1–10, not on a published corpus protocol. The Washington Post "favourite words" analysis (≈330,000 messages, surfaced via Omni Ekonomi) is **English data** and was deliberately not used to justify any Swedish vocabulary entry.

5. **Regional/Finland-Swedish varieties.** No source found on AI text patterns specific to Finland-Swedish or other regional varieties.

6. **"vital" / "oumbärlig" frequency.** Sourced as practitioner "ruined words" (SEO.se) rather than from a frequency count; included as Tier 1 on that practitioner basis only.
